The UK extremism and life experience survey

Conducted in 2019, a facebook ad survey investigated the demographic backgrounds, political leanings and belief systems of UK residents aged 16 and up. This dataset is publicly available on the Harvard Dataverse website here and contains 333 features with 4222 responses. In this first stage, I will conduct exploratory data analysis on this dataset to determine the various demographic distributions and overall correlations observed between political leanings and survey answers.

Extremism in the UK

One interesting component in this survey is that participants were asked 10 questions on violence with the intention of revealing their 'extremism' by taking a mean score from their ten answers. I believe that this term should be reworded as 'belief in the efficacy of violence' since based on the questions asked, the participant could not reveal extremist opinions - only the value of violence in reaching these goals. Therefore, for the purpose of this study, I will refer to 'extremism' - which is a very loaded term - as 'Belief in the efficacy of violence' since this more accurately summarises what participants were asked.

Political leanings were also taken during this survey as determined by governmental policy-related questions: I felt that these were accurately termed given the nature of the questions and so will continue to refer to this as 'Political Leaning'.

Throughout the survey, various demographic factors were measured including religion and ethnicity. For the purpose of data integrity, I will not draw associations between these factors and political leanings/answers to survey questions. These small-sample based associations are firstly not representative of large, complex societal groups and secondly may lead to harmful and misinformed judgements.
